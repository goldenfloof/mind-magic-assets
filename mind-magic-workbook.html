the Six Weeks

"At its heart, manifesting is a practise of wellbeing for orienting harmoniously with life. There is no end. As long as we are breathing, hoping, struggling + persisting, we are manifesting our lives." — Dr James R. Doty

This digital workbook follows your six-week Mind Magic programme. Each week builds upon the last, guiding you from reclaiming focus, through clarifying your vision, removing obstacles, embedding intention, pursuing passionately, and finally releasing with open hands. Use the journal spaces to record your observations, frustrations, progress, doubts and insights.

Week One / Step One

Reclaim Your Power
to Focus Your Mind

"Any man could, if he were so inclined, be the sculptor of his own brain." — Santiago Ramon y Cajal

i
What am I already manifesting?
Learning outcome: Clarity

Reflect on what you already have with appreciation. You have the power to purposefully influence the circumstances of your life. Note moments of joy and trust in what already exists around you.

Your reflections
ii
Relaxing the body
Learning outcome: Calm body = calm mind

Small actions can rewire the brain, creating habits that result in long-term changes. Rest and digest is important — to access clarity, we must first relax the body. Accessing the power within begins here.

Notes on your practice
iii
Build inner power (micro activities)
Learning outcome: Sense of agency

We have the agency to complete a task. There is power in tiny habits. Reflect on a simple activity you wish to do but haven't. Set an intention to complete it, then each time — whether done or not — congratulate yourself with: "I congratulate myself for completing this task."

Week Two / Step Two

Clarify What You
Truly Want

"There is no favourable wind for the sailor who does not know where to go." — Seneca the Younger

i
What does success look like for you?
Learning outcome: A jumping-off point for a lifetime vision

Keep what you write about success somewhere you'll see it often — taped to the fridge, above your desk, in your wallet. Consider making a recording, a collage, or sharing it with a trusted friend. This is a living vision you'll refine over your lifetime.

My vision of success
ii
Eliciting a positive emotion
Learning outcome: Pairing emotion with vision deepens brain salience

The experience of a strong positive emotion is part of how the brain decides what is important, worth pursuing, and deserving of attention. The more we pair a strongly felt emotion in the body with a mental image, the more resources the brain devotes to noticing it when it arises in the world.

Describe the emotion and where you feel it

Week Three / Step Three

Remove the Obstacles
in Your Mind

"The reality is that within each of us there is extraordinary power but we give that power away by our negative self-talk." — Dr James R. Doty

i
Beliefs and their opposites
Learning outcome: Consciously direct attention to where you choose

To manifest successfully, we must decrease the volume of the inner critic — the voice that tells us we can't. Consciously directing our attention to where we choose is the key to our inner power.

A limiting belief I hold
Its opposite — what could also be true
ii
Self-compassion
Learning outcome: Address feelings of unworthiness at the root

Self-compassion has the power to heal the parts of us that feel chronically unsafe and unloved, and transform them into vital sources of wisdom to share with others. Once we learn to be OK in the present moment, we can cultivate self-compassion — the inner critic's kryptonite.

A letter of compassion to yourself
Week Three Checklist
Revisit body relaxation practice
Continue micro activities
Revisit your vision of success
Identify and reframe a limiting belief
Write a self-compassion letter
Week Four / Step Four

Embed the Intention
in Your Subconscious

"Concentrate all your thoughts upon the work at hand. The sun's rays do not burn until brought to a focus." — Alexander Graham Bell

i
Visualising your intention
Learning outcome: Vivid mental pictures signal importance to the brain

The way to signal to the brain that your intention is important is by visualising it and accompanying it with a strongly felt positive emotion. The more vivid you can make your mental picture, the more the brain will respond as if it already lived through your intention becoming reality.

Describe your visualisation
ii
Bonus practice: Adding detail
Learning outcome: Enter a flow state in imagination

When we visualise our intention with full sensory detail, we strive to enter a flow state in imagination — as absorbed in imagined tasks as we would be in real life. When our physical environment takes up less space in consciousness, we give imagination free rein. This translates to complete alignment of conscious and subconscious minds.

Sensory details — what do you see, hear, smell, touch, taste?

Week Five / Step Five

Pursue Your Goal
Passionately

"Always go with your passions. Never ask yourself if it's realistic or not. Find the place inside yourself where nothing is impossible." — Deepak Chopra

i
How does my intention benefit life?
Learning outcome: Align intention with the life around you

Reflect on how your vision is part of something larger than you as an individual. One of the most powerful things we can do to increase inner power is to align our intention with the life around us. When facing increased doubts or resistance or lethargy, return to this statement for support and inspiration.

How my intention serves life beyond myself
ii
Scanning for synchronicity
Learning outcome: Increase receptivity to the hints your world offers

Making the most of synchronicity is a habit we can practise. As our subconscious begins tuning in to our environment for opportunities to realise our intention, surprising connections appear. Try this practise to increase your receptivity to the hints your world is offering.

Synchronicities I've noticed this week
7 days
Seven more days of visualisation
This week is all about repetition and momentum

Return to your Week 4 visualisation and spend seven more days seeing your intention in detail. The more you visualise and install it in the filing cabinet of your subconscious, the stronger your inner bloodhound. If your intention starts to materialise, be excited — but do not stop the practice.

Daily check-in — note shifts, insights, resistances
Week Five Checklist
All previous practices maintained
Written how intention benefits life
Scanning for synchronicity
Revisiting visualisation for 7 days
Week Six / Step Six

Release Expectations
+ Open to Magic

"Nothing important comes into being overnight; even grapes and figs need time to ripen." — Epictetus

i
Bonus practice: Gratitude Letter
Learning outcome: Shift focus from lack to what is already working

Cultivating gratitude is a simple, effective means to shift focus from what we still lack to what is already working and energising in our lives. Gratitude brightens the mind, attunes to the good, and softens frustration. When we savour the warmth, beauty, kindness and abundance we already have, it becomes easier to let go of attachment to a particular outcome.

My gratitude letter
ii
Let go of attachment and open to Magic
Learning outcome: Trust the inner compass to plot the next step

We rarely need to know how our intention will be realised, or how we will reach our destination. We only need to look for the next right step. If we are willing to attend carefully to the feedback from our environment and the people around us, we often discover our inner compass has already plotted the next step — whether we expected it or not.

What am I releasing? What am I opening to?
