Facilitation Guide

Practical guidance for mentors delivering the TTT programme, and for new trainers running their first MM sessions

The Facilitation Philosophy
The role of a MM trainer is not to perform expertise. It is to create the conditions under which someone else can encounter their own capacity for compassion.
This distinction matters. Expertise without warmth creates distance. Warmth without rigour creates a lovely experience that doesn't stick. Mind Magic holds both: the scientific backbone gives confidence, the compassion methodology gives warmth, and the trainer's job is to embody that synthesis.
For Every Session — The Opening

How to Begin

→Arrive settled before participants. Your nervous system sets the room's baseline.
→Begin with 2–3 minutes of breath or body awareness — no explanation needed, just lead it.
→Name the purpose of the session simply and honestly. No oversell.
→Acknowledge where participants are coming from (busy day, commute, whatever the group shares).
→Establish the container: what this space is for, and what stays in the room.
For Every Session — The Closing

How to End

→Leave 10 minutes for integration. Never end in practice — end in reflection.
→Invite one word or one sentence from the group. Don't require it.
→Signpost what comes next — participants feel safer knowing the arc.
→End with something that honours what happened: a line, a breath, a bow. Make it yours.
→Stay available for 5 minutes after. People often need a quiet word they couldn't say in the group.
Handling the Hard Moments

When Someone Is Overwhelmed

→Slow down first. Don't rush in. A calm, unhurried presence is the most powerful intervention.
→Name it gently. "I notice something's moving for you — take whatever space you need."
→Offer an anchor. A hand on the chest, a long exhale, feet on the floor — bring them into the body.
→Don't interpret. You're not their therapist. You don't need to know what it means — you just need to keep them safe.
→Check in after. A private message or a moment at the close of the session matters enormously.
→Know your referral pathway. Have three contacts ready for anyone who needs more than MM offers.
Handling Scepticism

The Science Question

→Scepticism is a gift — it means someone's paying attention. Welcome it.
→You don't need to win the argument. You need to open a door: "That's a fair challenge. Here's what the research shows — and I invite you to experience it yourself and draw your own conclusions."
→Jim's peer-reviewed research is your foundation — be specific, not vague. "CCARE studies at Stanford found that compassion training over 8 weeks produced measurable changes in empathic response and reduced physiological stress markers."
→Never oversell. The methodology speaks for itself when delivered with integrity.
Maintaining Your Own Practice

The Trainer's Practice

→You cannot reliably take people somewhere you haven't been yourself today — not years ago, today.
→Minimum: 15 minutes of personal practice on any day you deliver a session.
→Attend at least one MM group session per quarter as a participant, not a trainer.
→Use the MM community — other certified trainers are your best source of support, challenge, and inspiration.
→If you notice yourself delivering on autopilot, stop. Refresh before you deliver again.
Sample Session Architecture — 90 Minutes (Corporate Context)

The 90-Minute MM Session

0–10 min
Arrival & Grounding

Silent arrival, brief breath practice. No screens, no introductions yet. Let the room settle.

10–20 min
Frame & Science

Brief context: what we're doing and why it works. Neuroplasticity, compassion, the evidence. Keep it light — plant the seed, don't lecture.

20–55 min
Core Practice Sequence

The heart of the session. Guided body awareness → self-compassion practice → outward-directed compassion. Uninterrupted. Slow. Hold the silence.

55–75 min
Reflective Integration

Individual journalling (5 min), then optional sharing in pairs, then whole group. Facilitate, don't lead. Let the practice speak.

75–90 min
Close & Carry-Forward

One word from the group. Practical home practice (2 min per day until next session). Gratitude close. Stay available.

Competency Framework

The five domains assessed for MM Certified Trainer certification — and what mastery looks like in each

✦ Certification is awarded when a trainee demonstrates competence across all five domains. Each domain is assessed through a combination of the live teach session, the written facilitation plan, and practicum feedback. Completion of the programme is not sufficient — demonstrated competence is required.
🧠
Domain 1
Methodology Knowledge & Scientific Fluency
Foundation: Can explain the six-week MM methodology in sequence and articulate why each element is ordered as it is.
Foundation: Can describe the neuroscience of compassion and mindfulness practice in accessible language with reference to Jim's research at CCARE.
Mastery: Can respond to sceptical questions about the science with clarity, confidence, and appropriate humility — without overreaching into clinical claims.
Mastery: Can articulate what makes MM distinct from MBSR, Dispenza-style neuroscience coaching, and generic mindfulness — and why that distinction matters.
💜
Domain 2
Holding Space & Facilitation Presence
Foundation: Arrives settled and creates a grounded, safe container for participants from the first moment of a session.
Foundation: Paces delivery with awareness — slows down in moments of depth, holds silence without anxiety, resists the urge to fill every space with words.
Mastery: Responds to visible emotional activation in the room (tears, tension, resistance) with calm presence, appropriate acknowledgement, and skill — without projecting, interpreting, or over-managing.
Mastery: Their own embodied practice is visible in the quality of how they lead — not just in what they say, but in how they are.
🎯
Domain 3
Language, Communication & Contextual Adaptation
Foundation: Uses clear, warm, non-jargon language that meets participants where they are — adjusting vocabulary for different audiences without losing the essence of the methodology.
Foundation: Has produced a full facilitation plan adapted for their primary client group, demonstrating thoughtful contextual awareness.
Mastery: Can explain the MM methodology compellingly in under 60 seconds for a sceptical corporate audience, and in a completely different register for a therapeutic or personal development context.
Mastery: Knows what to flex in the methodology and what must remain fixed — and can articulate the reasoning for both.
🔄
Domain 4
Responsiveness & Group Dynamics
Foundation: Reads the room accurately — can identify when energy is low, resistance is present, or a participant needs particular attention.
Foundation: Adjusts the session in real time when needed — slowing down, offering alternatives, checking in — without losing the thread of the programme.
Mastery: Can facilitate a group debrief that deepens the practice rather than flattening it into intellectual discussion — holding both the felt experience and the cognitive reflection at once.
Mastery: Has a clear and demonstrable plan for when things go unexpectedly — participant distress, technical failure, group resistance — and can respond without destabilising themselves or the room.
⚖️
Domain 5
Ethics, Boundaries & Safeguarding
Foundation: Can clearly articulate the difference between what a MM certified trainer does and what a therapist, counsellor, or clinical practitioner does — and observes that boundary in practice.
Foundation: Has at least three appropriate referral contacts and knows when and how to use them.
Mastery: Delivers sessions with the MM brand accurately represented — neither underselling the science nor overclaiming outcomes. Protects the integrity of the methodology and the brand.
Mastery: Understands and honours the licence agreement, including what can and cannot be adapted, and how the MM trademark must be used.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions applicants and trainers most commonly ask

Before the Programme
Do I need to have completed the full MM Core Programme to apply? +
Yes. This is a non-negotiable prerequisite. The methodology cannot be taught by someone who hasn't lived it. Completing the Core Programme is not just a gate — it's the first step of your trainer formation. It means that when you lead a practice, you're speaking from experience, not instruction. This also means every applicant arrives with shared foundations, which makes the cohort experience richer for everyone.
I'm a therapist — do I need to adjust how I work with MM? +
The MM certification trains you as a mindfulness and compassion practitioner, not as a therapist using MM techniques in therapy. The distinction matters ethically and legally. Module 2.2 specifically addresses contextual adaptation for therapeutic settings, and we'll discuss how to hold the boundary between the two clearly and carefully. Many therapists find that offering MM as a separate, structured programme alongside their therapeutic work is more effective than attempting to integrate it within sessions.
What if I can't attend a live session? +
All live sessions are recorded. You may miss a maximum of one session per phase, provided you watch the recording and complete the associated workbook exercises before the next session. Attendance at the assessed practicum sessions in Phase 3 is mandatory and cannot be made up by recording. If you anticipate significant scheduling challenges, please discuss this with your mentor before the programme begins.
During the Programme
What happens if I don't pass the assessment? +
If you don't meet the competency standard in your first assessed teach session or written plan, you'll receive detailed written feedback from your mentor and one opportunity to resubmit or re-deliver within 60 days. This isn't a failure — it's the programme working as designed. Certification means something precisely because not everyone passes on the first attempt. If the resubmission is also unsuccessful, we'll have a conversation about whether additional support and a deferred cohort makes sense.
How much time should I expect to spend outside of live sessions? +
Plan for 4–6 hours per week during the 8-week programme: the 3-hour live session plus approximately 2–3 hours for workbook exercises, practice tasks, reading, and your own daily practice. The programme is designed for people already working full time — it is intensive, but structured to be manageable. The 30-day practicum period after the taught programme typically requires around 4 hours total preparation and delivery.
After Certification
Can I run my own TTT programme and certify my own trainers? +
No. The MM Certified Trainer licence allows you to deliver MM programmes to participants — it does not authorise you to certify others. The right to train and certify MM trainers sits exclusively with Mind Magic Inc. This protects the quality and consistency of the methodology globally, and preserves the value of the certification you've earned. If demand in your region grows to a point where a regional trainer hub makes sense, that's a conversation for MM leadership.
What happens if I let my licence lapse? +
If your annual licence is not renewed, you may no longer use the MM Certified Trainer credential or deliver sessions under the MM brand. You retain the learning and your own practice. To reinstate the licence, there's a £200 reinstatement fee and a short catch-up CPD module to bring you up to date with any methodology updates. If your licence lapses for more than two years, you'll be required to complete a re-certification pathway.
How much should I charge my own clients for MM programmes? +
Pricing is your decision — the licence does not set minimum or maximum prices. We provide benchmark guidance: comparable 6-week programmes in your market, the value of the credential, and the typical price range of your existing work. Most certified trainers integrate MM as either a standalone offering (typically £600–1,200 per participant for a 6-week programme) or as a premium add-on to their existing coaching or therapy practice. The annual CPD sessions include a session specifically on marketing and pricing MM for your context.
Is there a community for certified trainers? +
Yes — and it's one of the most valued parts of the licence. The MM Certified Trainer community is a private network of practitioners who share the same credential and commitment. It's a space for support, peer supervision, resource sharing, and collective development of the methodology. MM runs four live CPD sessions per year for certified trainers, plus regular community events. Isolation is the enemy of a good practice — the community exists to make sure no certified trainer has to figure this out alone.
